Test Claims Like a Scientist

In this project, you will investigate a climate‑related claim using peer‑reviewed research. Your goal is not persuasion — it is scientific evaluation. Build a clear falsification test: What evidence would prove this claim wrong?

Project Requirements

Group Structure

  • Teams of 3–4 students
  • 8–10 minute presentation
  • 10–12 slide deck
  • Every member must speak

Minimum Source Quality

  • At least 2 peer‑reviewed sources
  • Use academic databases or discovery tools
  • Cite every figure and dataset

Research Questions (Choose One)

Falsifiability

Why is falsifiability essential in science?

Predictions & Testing

What predictions has global warming theory made, and how are they tested?

Challenges

What evidence could potentially challenge aspects of climate theory?

Scientific Responses

How do scientists respond to critiques of climate models?

Historical Revisions

Have climate-related hypotheses ever been revised or falsified?

Bias

How does confirmation bias affect public understanding?

Required Falsification Slide

  • Prediction: If our claim is true, we should observe ______.
  • Falsifier: If we instead observe ______, the claim would be wrong or need revision.
  • Dataset: Which measurable data could test this?
A claim that cannot be wrong in principle is not scientific.

Recommended Research Tools

  • Google Scholar – citation tracking
