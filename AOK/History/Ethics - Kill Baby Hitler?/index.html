The Watcher Times — Lesson Guide

A TOK-style historical simulation about ethical decision-making and causality. Students play “The Watcher” and confront the classic counterfactual: would you prevent a future mass-harm catalyst in infancy? The engine tracks structural pressures (zeitgeist) and individual agency (Great Person) through a branching timeline toward 2025.

Learning goals

  • Compare Great Person explanations of history with structural/zeitgeist explanations.
  • Evaluate ethical reasoning frameworks under uncertainty (tradeoffs, unintended consequences).
  • Reflect on responsibility for action vs inaction in morally loaded counterfactuals.

Teacher setup (5 minutes)

  • Open a new ChatGPT chat (or your preferred LLM chat).
  • Download or copy the master prompt below and paste it as the first message.
  • Tell students: “Follow the format: Choice X — Ethical frame — one-sentence justification.”
  • Optional: run in pairs (one chooses, one justifies), then swap roles.

Student instructions (in class)

Core loop: Read the “Newswire” → pick a numbered choice → justify with one ethical frame.

  • Consequentialist: minimize total harm.
  • Deontological: some actions are wrong regardless of outcomes.
  • Non-interventionist: you lack legitimacy to rewrite lives.
  • Tragic responsibility: no clean hands; choose the least-worst.

Response format:

Debrief questions (10–15 minutes)

  • Which outcomes seemed driven by individuals vs structural pressures? Why?
  • Did “entropy” (history realigning) change how you judged interventions?
  • Did your ethical framework stay consistent? If it shifted, what justified the shift?
  • What counts as “making things worse” in a world of tradeoffs?
Classroom note: keep discussion analytical and ethical; avoid sensational details. The point is reasoning about causality and responsibility.
